Jason's home on the Internet

It looks this way on purpose.

Why does it look like this, though?

The modern web has very sleek and polished aesthetic, but also very standardized. There's a nostalgic place in my heart for the earlier web -- a place of weirdness, disorder and chaos. Traveling the web should feel like going on a new and exciting adventure, but a lot of the time it feels like the equivalent of traveling across the globe and visiting a Starbucks identical to the one near my house.

Was the older web better?

The web used to look like every person was given the same box of LEGOs and they arranged those shared blocks in different ways that communicated something about them. While it was a less polished experience, it was one that felt more human because much more of the design was visible to us. A lot of the web today feels like it came off an assembly line or was built by a corporate committee.

What's the point of this?

For me to have fun. The web is a delightful and magical place! Our websites should reflect our joys.
