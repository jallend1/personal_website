Jason's home on the Internet

It looks this way on purpose.

The modern web has very sleek and polished aesthetic, but it's also very standardized. The earlier web felt weirder, disordered and more unpredictable. Visiting a new web page feels like going to a new Starbucks -- You know exactly what to expect.

Was the older web better?

It struck me as more human. The web used to look like every person was given the same box of LEGOs and they arranged those shared blocks in different ways that communicated something about them. A lot of the web today feels like it came off an assembly line or was built by a corporate committee.

What's the point of this?

Primarily for me to have fun. The web is a delightful and magical place! Our websites should reflect our joys.

And an era where websites were just the Dancing Baby GIF from Ally McBeal and you didn't have to worry about whether your visit to the site was facilitating genocide in Myanmar or eroding democracy was simply a more joyous time to be online.
